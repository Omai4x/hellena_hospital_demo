Emergency Services

Our emergency team is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. If you are facing a medical emergency, call us immediately or come directly to the hospital.

Medical Emergency?

Do not wait. Call us immediately or proceed directly to Hellena Hospital at 60B Gas Line, Ohuru Isimiri, Aba — at any hour of the day or night.

When to Come to Emergency

  • Chest pain or suspected heart attack
  • Sudden severe headache or signs of stroke
  • Serious accident, trauma, or injury
  • Heavy uncontrolled bleeding
  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • High fever with convulsions (especially in children)
  • Pregnancy complications — heavy bleeding, sudden pain
  • Loss of consciousness or unresponsiveness
  • Suspected poisoning or overdose

What To Do While Coming

  • Call us ahead so we can prepare for your arrival
  • Keep the patient calm and still
  • Bring any medications the patient currently takes
  • Have someone drive — never drive yourself in an emergency

What NOT To Do

  • Do not wait to see if symptoms improve — act immediately
  • Do not give food, water, or medicine before a doctor sees the patient
  • Do not leave an unconscious patient alone at any time
First Aid Basics

While Waiting for Emergency Care

CPR (Basic)

  1. Check the person is unresponsive
  2. Call for help immediately
  3. Place heel of hand on centre of chest
  4. Push hard & fast — 100–120 compressions/min
  5. Continue until help arrives

Severe Bleeding

  1. Apply firm, direct pressure to wound
  2. Use a clean cloth or bandage
  3. Do not remove the cloth — add more on top
  4. Elevate the injured limb if possible
  5. Get to hospital immediately

Burns

  1. Cool burn with running water 10–20 min
  2. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste
  3. Cover loosely with clean bandage
  4. Do not burst blisters
  5. Seek care for all but very minor burns

Choking (Adult)

  1. Encourage forceful coughing
  2. Give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades
  3. Give 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre)
  4. Alternate back blows and thrusts
  5. Call for help if unresolved quickly

Stroke — Act FAST

  1. Face — is one side drooping?
  2. Arms — can they raise both arms?
  3. Speech — is it slurred or confused?
  4. Time — call for help immediately
  5. Do not give food or water

Pregnancy Emergency

  1. Keep the woman calm, lying on her left side
  2. Note time and nature of symptoms
  3. Do not attempt any internal examination
  4. Call Hellena Hospital immediately
  5. Bring to maternity unit without delay
