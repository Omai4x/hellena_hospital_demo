Patient Information

Everything you need to know before, during, and after your visit to Hellena Hospital.

What are the hospital's opening hours? +

Hellena Hospital operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, including public holidays. Scheduled outpatient clinics run 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM weekdays and 9:00 AM – 2:00 PM Saturdays.

Do I need an appointment? +

Walk-in patients are welcome at any time. However, booking in advance is recommended to reduce waiting time. Call +2349164474223 or +2348026269356.

What should I bring on my first visit? +

Bring a valid ID, any previous medical records or test results, a list of current medications, and your health insurance card if applicable.

Do you accept health insurance? +

We are expanding our insurance partnerships. Please call our front desk to enquire about your specific insurer before your visit.

What payment methods do you accept? +

We accept cash and bank transfers. Speak to our billing desk upon arrival.

Are visiting hours restricted for inpatients? +

Standard visiting hours are 10:00 AM – 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM – 7:00 PM daily. These may vary by ward. Check with the nursing station on arrival.

Is parking available? +

Yes. Parking is available on the hospital premises at 60B Gas Line, Ohuru Isimiri. Our security team will direct you on arrival.

How Admission Works

We aim to make the admission process as smooth and stress-free as possible.

1

Arrive at Reception

Come to the front desk. Our staff will take your details and guide you through registration. Bring your ID and any relevant medical documents.

2

Triage / Initial Assessment

A nurse will assess your condition to determine priority of care. Emergency cases are seen immediately; routine cases are seen in order of arrival.

3

Doctor Consultation

A qualified doctor will conduct a thorough examination and recommend the appropriate course of action — tests, medication, or admission.

4

Investigations (if required)

If tests are needed, our in-house lab runs diagnostics. Results for most standard tests are available within the same visit.

5

Treatment & Ward Assignment

If inpatient care is needed, you'll be assigned to the appropriate ward and a care plan initiated immediately.

6

Billing & Discharge

Our billing team works with you transparently. Discharge planning begins early so you can transition home safely and confidently.

Your Rights as a Patient

Hellena Hospital is committed to upholding the dignity and rights of every patient.

Right to Respectful Care

Receive care that is considerate and free from discrimination based on age, gender, tribe, religion, or socioeconomic status.

Right to Information

Receive complete, understandable information about your diagnosis, treatment options, and prognosis to make informed decisions.

Right to Confidentiality

Your medical information is strictly confidential. We will not disclose your details without consent, except where required by law.

Right to Refuse Treatment

You may refuse any procedure or treatment, after being fully informed of the consequences of that decision.

Right to Privacy

You are entitled to privacy during examinations, procedures, and discussions about your health.

Right to Complain

If dissatisfied with any aspect of your care, you have the right to raise a complaint with our Patient Relations desk at any time.

Preparing for Your Visit

A little preparation helps us give you the best possible care.

For a General Consultation +

Write down your symptoms, when they started, and any medications you currently take. Bring any previous test results or doctor's reports.

For Laboratory Tests +

Some blood tests require fasting for 8–12 hours. Your referring doctor will specify if this applies. Drink plenty of water unless told otherwise.

For Antenatal (ANC) Appointments +

Bring your ANC card if you have one. Wear comfortable clothing. A support person is welcome to accompany you.

For Surgical Procedures +

Follow pre-operative instructions from your surgeon. Generally, avoid food and drink for 6–8 hours beforehand. Arrange for someone to accompany you.

For Paediatric Visits +

Bring your child's immunization card and health records. Note symptoms clearly. A familiar toy can help keep the child calm.
